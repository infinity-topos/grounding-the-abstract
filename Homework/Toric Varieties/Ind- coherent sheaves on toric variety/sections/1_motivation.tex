\section{Introduction}


We focus on non-normal toric varieties exhibiting non-rational singularities. 
In such cases, the blow-up of a singular point fails to satisfy the favorable properties found in the rational setting. 
Specifically, the resulting cohomological obstruction becomes significantly more intricate, which inevitably impacts the canonical definitions of heights and measures.

In this paper, we introduce the framework of ind-coherent sheaves on toric varieties and employ the Ishida complex to provide an explicit combinatorial computation. 
The final part of this work compares the classical blow-up approach with the ind-coherent sheaf method in the context of defining heights and measures for singular toric varieties.

While $\mathrm{IndCoh}(X)$ is formally rooted in $\infty$-categories, we adopt a "hands-on" model via the Ind-completion of the dg-enhancement of $D^b(\mathrm{Coh}(X))$. 
This ensures that all mapping spaces are explicitly structured as dg-complexes, preserving the full homological data of the Ishida complex without requiring higher-categorical machinery.

Throughout this paper, we work over an algebraically closed field $k$ of characteristic zero.

\begin{abstract}
This note gives an expository account of the toric coherent--constructible
correspondence, mainly based on \cite{FLTZ09}. The main focus is the sheaf-theoretic part of the correspondence: on one side, coherent sheaves on a toric variety; on the other side, constructible sheaves on a real vector space determined by the fan. We explain how the combinatorial data of a toric variety controls both sides and how the correspondence is proved by comparing their natural families of generators.

Besides the sheaf-theoretic proof, this note also includes two supplementary parts. Following the discussion in \cite{FLTZ09}, we briefly recall the Fukaya-categorical interpretation of the toric CCC, explaining how the constructible side may be viewed through microlocal sheaf theory and Fukaya categories. In the final part, based mainly on \cite{FLTZDM,Kuw20,FLTZFM,HuSuh23}, we give a short overview of several related generalizations, including toric stacks, toric fibrations, and Fourier--Mukai-type constructions.
\end{abstract}

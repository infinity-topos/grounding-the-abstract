
\section{Introduction}
\subsection{Main Aim and Strategy}
The coherent--constructible correspondence is a bridge between two different
types of sheaf theories. On one side, one studies equivariant coherent sheaves
on a toric variety. On the other side, one studies constructible sheaves on a
real vector space, with singular support restricted by a conic subset determined
by the fan. The main point of the correspondence is that the algebraic and
equivariant data of a toric variety can be translated into polyhedral and
microlocal data on the constructible side.

The main goal of this paper is to explain and prove the coherent--constructible
correspondence for smooth toric varieties. More precisely, if \(X_\Sigma\) is a
smooth toric variety with acting torus \(T\), character lattice \(M\), and fan
\(\Sigma\subset N_{\mathbb R}\), then the correspondence takes the form of a
quasi-equivalence of dg categories
\[
\kappa_\Sigma:\mathrm{Perf}_T(X_\Sigma)
\overset{\sim}{\longrightarrow}
Sh_{\Lambda_\Sigma}(M_{\mathbb R}).
\]
Here \(\mathrm{Perf}_T(X_\Sigma)\) is the dg category of \(T\)-equivariant
perfect complexes on \(X_\Sigma\), and \(Sh_{\Lambda_\Sigma}(M_{\mathbb R})\)
is the dg category of constructible sheaves on \(M_{\mathbb R}\) whose singular
support is contained in the FLTZ skeleton \(\Lambda_\Sigma\). The FLTZ skeleton
is a conic subset of \(T^*M_{\mathbb R}\) defined directly from the fan
\(\Sigma\).

The proof follows the standard generator strategy for dg categories. The key point is that, in the toric setting, both sides admit explicit families of generators: the coherent theta objects on the coherent side and the constructible theta sheaves on the constructible side. These generators are indexed by the same combinatorial data coming from the fan. Therefore, the equivalence is not proved by comparing arbitrary objects directly. Instead, it is reduced to comparing the Hom-complexes between the two families of theta generators. Once these Hom-complexes are identified and the generation results are established on both sides, the coherent--constructible correspondence follows from the generator criterion.
\subsection{Organization of the Note}
The organization of the paper is as follows. Section 2 recalls the symplectic and Kähler background of toric varieties, especially the relation between ample toric line bundles and moment polytopes. Section 3 fixes the categorical and sheaf-theoretic conventions used later, including dg categories, equivariant coherent sheaves, constructible sheaves, and singular support.

Sections 4, 5, and 6 form the main part of the paper. Section 4 describes the coherent side and introduces the coherent theta generators. Section 5 constructs the constructible side through the FLTZ skeleton and the constructible theta sheaves. Section 6 proves the toric coherent--constructible correspondence by comparing the Hom-complexes between these generators.

The remaining sections are supplementary. Section 7 briefly discusses the Fukaya-categorical interpretation of the constructible side, following the perspective of \cite{FLTZ09}. Section 8 presents several explicit examples of the toric CCC; these computations are based on the general framework of \cite{FLTZ09}. Section 9 gives a short overview of related generalizations: toric Deligne--Mumford stacks as in \cite{FLTZDM}, nonequivariant toric stacks as in \cite{Kuw20}, Fourier--Mukai-type constructions as in \cite{FLTZFM}, and toric fibrations as in \cite{HuSuh23}.

\textbf{Readers who are already familiar with dg categories, constructible sheaves, singular support, and the basic symplectic background of toric varieties may start directly from Section 4.}
\subsection{Standard Notation}
We use the standard notation of toric geometry. Let \(T\) be an algebraic torus
over \(\mathbb{C}\). We write
\[
N=\operatorname{Hom}(\mathbb{C}^{*},T),
\qquad
M=\operatorname{Hom}(T,\mathbb{C}^{*})
\]
for the cocharacter and character lattices. Thus
\(M=\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(N,\mathbb{Z})\). Their realifications are
denoted by
\[
N_{\mathbb{R}}=N\otimes_{\mathbb{Z}}\mathbb{R},
\qquad
M_{\mathbb{R}}=M\otimes_{\mathbb{Z}}\mathbb{R}.
\]

For a fan \(\Sigma\subset N_{\mathbb{R}}\), we denote by \(X_{\Sigma}\) the
associated toric variety. If \(\sigma\in\Sigma\) is a cone, then its dual cone is
\[
\sigma^{\vee}
=
\{m\in M_{\mathbb{R}}\mid \langle m,n\rangle\ge 0
\text{ for all } n\in\sigma\}.
\]
The corresponding affine toric chart is written as
\[
U_{\sigma}=\operatorname{Spec}\mathbb{C}[\sigma^{\vee}\cap M],
\]
and its inclusion into \(X_{\Sigma}\) is denoted by
\[
i_{\sigma}:U_{\sigma}\hookrightarrow X_{\Sigma}.
\]
For a cone \(\sigma\), we write
\[
\sigma^\vee=\{m\in M_{\mathbb R}\mid \langle m,n\rangle\ge 0
\text{ for all }n\in\sigma\}
\]
for its dual cone, and
\[
\sigma^\perp=\{m\in M_{\mathbb R}\mid \langle m,n\rangle=0
\text{ for all }n\in\sigma\}
\]
for its annihilator. We also use
\[
M_\sigma=M/(\sigma^\perp\cap M).
\]

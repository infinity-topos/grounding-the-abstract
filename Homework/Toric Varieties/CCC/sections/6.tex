\section{The Toric Coherent--Constructible Correspondence}
We now state the toric coherent--constructible correspondence. Let
\(X_\Sigma\) be a smooth toric variety with acting torus \(T\), and let
\(\Lambda_\Sigma\subset T^*M_{\mathbb R}\) be the FLTZ skeleton associated to
the fan \(\Sigma\). The coherent side is the equivariant perfect category
\(\operatorname{Perf}_T(X_\Sigma)\), which agrees with
\(D^b(\operatorname{Coh}_T(X_\Sigma))\) in the smooth case. The
constructible side is the dg category of constructible sheaves on
\(M_{\mathbb R}\) with singular support contained in \(\Lambda_\Sigma\).

\begin{theorem}[Toric coherent--constructible correspondence]
Let \(X_\Sigma\) be a smooth toric variety. There is a quasi-equivalence of
dg categories
\[
\kappa_\Sigma:
\operatorname{Perf}_T(X_\Sigma)
\xrightarrow{\sim}
Sh_{\Lambda_\Sigma}(M_{\mathbb R}).
\]
Equivalently, on homotopy categories one obtains an equivalence
\[
D^b(\operatorname{Coh}_T(X_\Sigma))
\simeq
H^0\bigl(Sh_{\Lambda_\Sigma}(M_{\mathbb R})\bigr).
\]
\end{theorem}

The proof will be given by comparing the theta generators on the two sides.
The objects \(\Theta'(\sigma,\chi)\) and \(\Theta(\sigma,\chi)\) are indexed
by the same data \((\sigma,\chi)\), and the functor \(\kappa_\Sigma\) is
characterized by
\[
\kappa_\Sigma\bigl(\Theta'(\sigma,\chi)\bigr)=\Theta(\sigma,\chi).
\]
It remains to compare the Hom-complexes between these generators and then
apply the generator criterion for quasi-equivalences.


\subsection{The Correspondence of Generators}

The theta objects on the two sides are indexed by the same combinatorial
data. More precisely, for each cone \(\sigma\in\Sigma\), let
\(q_\sigma:M\to M_\sigma\) be the quotient map.

\begin{lemma}
Let \(\sigma\in\Sigma\), and let \(\chi,\chi'\in M\). If
\(q_\sigma(\chi)=q_\sigma(\chi')\), then
\(\Theta'(\sigma,\chi)\simeq \Theta'(\sigma,\chi')\).
Hence the coherent-side theta object depends only on the class
\(\bar\chi\in M_\sigma\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The condition \(q_\sigma(\chi)=q_\sigma(\chi')\) means that
\(\chi'-\chi\in\sigma^\perp\cap M\). Put \(m_0=\chi'-\chi\). Then the
monomial \(\chi^{m_0}\) is an invertible regular function on \(U_\sigma\).
Multiplication by \(\chi^{m_0}\) gives a \(T\)-equivariant isomorphism
\(\mathcal O_{U_\sigma}(\chi)\simeq\mathcal O_{U_\sigma}(\chi')\), and
pushing forward along \(i_\sigma:U_\sigma\hookrightarrow X_\Sigma\) gives
\(\Theta'(\sigma,\chi)\simeq\Theta'(\sigma,\chi')\).
\end{proof}

Thus we may write the coherent-side theta object as
\(\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi)\), where \(\bar\chi\in M_\sigma\). The
constructible theta object \(\Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi)\) is already indexed by
the same data. The correspondence of generators is therefore
\[
\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi)\longmapsto \Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi).
\]

The functor \(\kappa_\Sigma\), once constructed, will be characterized on
theta generators by
\[
\kappa_\Sigma\bigl(\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi)\bigr)
=
\Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi).
\]
The next step is to compare the Hom-complexes between these generators. This
comparison is the essential computation from which the CCC functor and the
quasi-equivalence will follow.
\subsection{Hom-Complex Computations}
We now compare the Hom-complexes between theta objects. For
\(\bar\chi\in M_\sigma\), choose a lift \(\chi\in M\) and write
\(P_{\sigma,\bar\chi}:=\chi+\sigma^\vee\). This subset is independent of the
chosen lift.

\begin{proposition}[Hom-complex comparison]
Let \(\sigma,\tau\in\Sigma\), \(\bar\chi\in M_\sigma\), and
\(\bar\psi\in M_\tau\). Then there is a natural quasi-isomorphism
\[
\operatorname{RHom}_T
\bigl(\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi),\Theta'(\tau,\bar\psi)\bigr)
\simeq
\operatorname{RHom}
\bigl(\Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi),\Theta(\tau,\bar\psi)\bigr).
\]
More explicitly, both sides are quasi-isomorphic to
\[
\begin{cases}
k, & P_{\sigma,\bar\chi}\subset P_{\tau,\bar\psi},\\
0, & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
\]
In the nonzero case, the complex is concentrated in degree zero.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
We compute the two sides separately. On the coherent side, a morphism
\(\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi)\to\Theta'(\tau,\bar\psi)\) is determined by an
equivariant monomial map between the corresponding equivariant line bundles
on the relevant toric affine charts. With our linearization convention, the
possible equivariant map is multiplication by the monomial of weight
\(\chi-\psi\). This multiplication map is regular precisely when
\(P_{\sigma,\bar\chi}\subset P_{\tau,\bar\psi}\). If this inclusion holds,
the space of equivariant maps is one-dimensional; if it does not hold, there
is no equivariant map. Since the relevant toric intersections are affine, no
higher derived Hom appears. Hence
\[
\operatorname{RHom}_T
\bigl(\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi),\Theta'(\tau,\bar\psi)\bigr)
\simeq
\begin{cases}
k, & P_{\sigma,\bar\chi}\subset P_{\tau,\bar\psi},\\
0, & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
\]

On the constructible side, the theta objects are costandard sheaves on the
closed convex polyhedra \(P_{\sigma,\bar\chi}\) and
\(P_{\tau,\bar\psi}\). For closed convex polyhedra \(P,Q\subset M_{\mathbb R}\),
one has
\(\operatorname{RHom}((i_P)_*\omega_P,(i_Q)_*\omega_Q)\simeq k\) if
\(P\subset Q\), and zero otherwise. This follows from Verdier duality:
\((i_P)_*\omega_P\simeq D(k_P)\), so the above Hom-complex is dual to the
corresponding Hom-complex between the constant sheaves \(k_Q\) and \(k_P\).
For convex polyhedra this complex is the cohomology of a contractible
polyhedral region when \(P\subset Q\), and is acyclic otherwise.

Applying this with \(P=P_{\sigma,\bar\chi}\) and
\(Q=P_{\tau,\bar\psi}\), we get
\[
\operatorname{RHom}
\bigl(\Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi),\Theta(\tau,\bar\psi)\bigr)
\simeq
\begin{cases}
k, & P_{\sigma,\bar\chi}\subset P_{\tau,\bar\psi},\\
0, & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
\]
Therefore the coherent and constructible Hom-complexes agree.

The comparison is compatible with composition: if
\(P_{\sigma,\bar\chi}\subset P_{\tau,\bar\psi}\subset
P_{\upsilon,\bar\eta}\), then the generator of the first Hom-complex
composes with the generator of the second to give the generator of the third.
If one of the inclusions fails, the corresponding Hom-complex is zero. Hence
the quasi-isomorphisms above are compatible with composition.
\end{proof}
\subsection{The CCC Functor}

We now construct the functor realizing the correspondence. Let
\(\mathcal A_\Sigma'\) be the full dg subcategory of
\(D(\operatorname{QCoh}_T(X_\Sigma))\) whose objects are the coherent-side
theta objects \(\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi)\). Similarly, let
\(\mathcal A_\Sigma\) be the full dg subcategory of
\(Sh_{\Lambda_\Sigma}(M_{\mathbb R})\) whose objects are the constructible
theta objects \(\Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi)\).

Define a map on objects by
\[
\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi)\longmapsto \Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi).
\]
By the Hom-complex comparison in Proposition 5.2, for every pair of indices
\((\sigma,\bar\chi)\) and \((\tau,\bar\psi)\), there is a natural
quasi-isomorphism
\[
\operatorname{RHom}_T
\bigl(\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi),\Theta'(\tau,\bar\psi)\bigr)
\simeq
\operatorname{RHom}
\bigl(\Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi),\Theta(\tau,\bar\psi)\bigr).
\]
These quasi-isomorphisms are compatible with composition. Hence the
assignment on objects extends to a dg functor
\[
\kappa_\Sigma^0:\mathcal A_\Sigma'\longrightarrow \mathcal A_\Sigma.
\]

The functor \(\kappa_\Sigma^0\) is fully faithful up to quasi-isomorphism on
the theta subcategories. To obtain the full CCC functor, we extend
\(\kappa_\Sigma^0\) to the triangulated dg subcategories generated by the
theta objects. Since exact dg functors preserve shifts, cones, and finite
direct sums, the assignment extends to an exact dg functor
\[
\kappa_\Sigma:
\big\langle \Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi)\mid
\sigma\in\Sigma,\ \bar\chi\in M_\sigma
\big\rangle
\longrightarrow
\big\langle \Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi)\mid
\sigma\in\Sigma,\ \bar\chi\in M_\sigma
\big\rangle .
\]

Equivalently, \(\kappa_\Sigma\) is the exact dg functor characterized by
\[
\kappa_\Sigma\bigl(\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi)\bigr)
=
\Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi)
\]
on theta generators, together with the Hom-complex identifications from
Proposition 5.2. By construction, \(\kappa_\Sigma\) is quasi-fully faithful
on the generating theta objects. The remaining step is to use the generation
results to prove that this functor is a quasi-equivalence.

\subsection{Proof of the Quasi-Equivalence}


We now prove that the functor constructed above is a quasi-equivalence. By
construction, \(\kappa_\Sigma\) sends each coherent-side theta object to the
corresponding constructible theta object:
\[
\kappa_\Sigma\bigl(\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi)\bigr)
=
\Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi).
\]
Moreover, Proposition 5.2 shows that, for any
\(\sigma,\tau\in\Sigma\), \(\bar\chi\in M_\sigma\), and
\(\bar\psi\in M_\tau\), the induced map on Hom-complexes
\[
\operatorname{RHom}_T
\bigl(\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi),\Theta'(\tau,\bar\psi)\bigr)
\longrightarrow
\operatorname{RHom}
\bigl(\Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi),\Theta(\tau,\bar\psi)\bigr)
\]
is a quasi-isomorphism.

It remains to check generation. On the coherent side, the theta objects
\(\Theta'(\sigma,\bar\chi)\) generate the ambient equivariant
quasi-coherent category. Passing to compact objects gives the perfect
equivariant category \(\operatorname{Perf}_T(X_\Sigma)\). Since
\(X_\Sigma\) is smooth, this category is a dg enhancement of
\(D^b(\operatorname{Coh}_T(X_\Sigma))\). On the constructible side,
Theorem 4.6 says that the theta objects
\(\Theta(\sigma,\bar\chi)\) generate
\(Sh_{\Lambda_\Sigma}(M_{\mathbb R})\).

Thus the hypotheses of the generator criterion for quasi-equivalences are
satisfied: the functor is exact, it identifies the Hom-complexes between
generators up to quasi-isomorphism, and the images of the generators
generate the target. Therefore \(\kappa_\Sigma\) is a quasi-equivalence:
\[
\kappa_\Sigma:
\operatorname{Perf}_T(X_\Sigma)
\xrightarrow{\sim}
Sh_{\Lambda_\Sigma}(M_{\mathbb R}).
\]
Equivalently, passing to homotopy categories gives
\[
D^b(\operatorname{Coh}_T(X_\Sigma))
\simeq
H^0\bigl(Sh_{\Lambda_\Sigma}(M_{\mathbb R})\bigr).
\]
This proves the toric coherent--constructible correspondence.
